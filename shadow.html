Shadow Path Mechanic

A puzzle exploration prototype where the player can only move inside shadows.

Overview

Shadow Path is an experimental mechanic prototype built in Unity. The core idea: the player can move only inside shadows, and must manipulate the world to cast new paths. This demo consists of three small handcrafted levels, each introducing a new mechanic layered on top of the previous one.

Level 1 — Rotating Stage & Time‑of‑Day Control

The first level focuses on teaching the player how shadow-based traversal works. The core interactions are:

  • A / D: Rotate the entire stage to change how obstacles cast shadows.
  • W / S: Shift the time of day, altering the sun angle and shadow length.
  • Movement is allowed only inside the shaded areas.

This level demonstrates the foundational logic: shadow projection, directional light manipulation, and boundary-based player movement.

Level 2 — Advanced Shadow Navigation

In the second level, the environment layout emphasizes a different use of the mechanic. Instead of simple rotations, the player must strategically combine lighting changes and environmental geometry to reach distant platforms.

This level showcases more complex shadow logic and how simple geometry can create emergent puzzle paths.

Level 3 — Expanding Tower Mechanic

The final level introduces a new system: an expandable tower that rises to connect shadow paths. The player must extend or contract the tower while adjusting the sunlight angle to cast a continuous shaded walkway leading to the goal.

This demonstrates the layering of mechanics and system interactions — dynamic geometry + real‑time lighting.
